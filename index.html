Sarah Brogden Payne


I am a rising junior at the University of Pennsylvania majoring in Linguistics and Computer and Information Science. Broadly, I am interested in the parallels between how language is processed by humans and by computers and how we can use cognitive science and psycholinguistics to inform Natural Language Processing (NLP). I work in several related labs at Penn.
In my free time, I tutor at the Mark's Family Writing Center and sit on the board of several of Penn's anti-violence organizations, including Abuse and Sexual Assault Prevention (ASAP). I also enjoy working on open-source code, running, listening to music, debating politics, and exploring Philadelphia with my friends.
I'm originally from Bloomington, Indiana, but I spent much of my childhood in the United Kingdom, including Suffolk, Bristol, and the greater London area.
