Sarah Brogden Payne

Sɛɹə Bɹɒgdɪn Pɛjn


I am an undergraduate at the University of Pennsylvania majoring in Linguistics and Computer and Information Science, graduating in May 2022. Broadly, I am interested in the use of Cognitive Science and Computational Psycholinguistics to design better Natural Language Processing (NLP) tools, as well as the use of computational cognitive modelling to better understand language acquisition and processing. My research focuses on both the cognitive and computational sides of this problem.

In my free time, I am a teaching assistant for Scalable and Cloud Computing (NETS212). I also sit on the board of several of Penn's anti-violence organizations; a particularly nice coverage of our work can be found in this 34th Street article. When I'm not working on any of the above, you can find me cooking, running, debating politics, writing open-source code, or listening to music.

I'm originally from Bloomington, Indiana, but I spent much of my childhood in the United Kingdom, including Suffolk, Bristol, and Surrey.
