Sarah Brogden Payne

/sɛɹə bɹɒgdɪn pɛjn/ (she/her)

Hello! I'm a senior at the University of Pennsylvania studying Linguistics and Computer Science with a minor in Cognitive Science. My interests are broadly in language acquisition, especially morphological and syntactic, and I develop algorithmic accounts of how children learn their native language. You can read more about my research here.

I am deeply passionate about increasing inclusion in academia. I have served as a teaching assistant for four courses in Penn's CIS department, and as a teaching assistant trainer where I teach new TAs about practices of inclusive teaching and student support. I was the 2021 chair of Penn's Abuse and Sexual Assault Prevention Group, and I currently represent survivors of sexual violence on Penn's University Council.

I'm originally from Bloomington, IN, but also grew up in England, including Bristol, Suffolk, and Surrey. My phonology is a complete mess as a result!
